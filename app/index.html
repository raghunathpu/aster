The Problem We Solve

Bengaluru handles 7 million+ vehicle trips per day. When disruptions occur — a bus breakdown on Mysore Road at 8 AM, a protest blocking ORR East during evening peak, water logging under Hebbal — the response is reactive, experience-driven, and unquantified.

ASTER classifies every incoming event as Low, Medium, or High impact, then generates a complete operational response plan in under 2 seconds.

How It Works

StepWhat Happens
1. Event LoggedOfficer reports incident via ASTRAM

3. Context EscalationRules check peak hour, junction hotspot, corridor priority
4. Response Plan GeneratedManpower, barricading, diversion, deployment SLA

Key Operational Findings

🔴 Corridor = Priority Signal
Named corridors are always High priority. This single feature accounts for 53.7% of model importance — the strongest single predictive signal for event severity.
🚗 Vehicle Breakdowns Dominate (But Aren't the Worst)
4,896 breakdowns (60%) are mostly Medium impact. BMTC buses take 47.8 min median to resolve — slowest vehicle type, disproportionate corridor risk.
⏰ Overnight Logging Spike Requires Normalisation
0–5 AM shows 30% of all event volume. Patrol officers log overnight incidents at shift start. Real-time deployment must normalise timestamps.
🌧️ Water Logging Clusters on ORR During Monsoon
Water logging events cluster on ORR (East/North) and known underpass locations. Pre-monsoon pre-positioning of drainage response is viable.
🎭 Planned Events Severely Underreported at 5.7%
Construction, VIP movements, public events need 24–48 hour lead-time classification for proper pre-positioning. Biggest workflow gap.
⚡ Accidents Resolve Fastest Despite High Disruption
Accidents have ~40 min median resolution — fastest of all High-impact causes. Emergency protocols and CATS coordination work. General congestion lingers longest (90+ min).

🔍 Target Engineering — Transparency Note

ASTER's impact_tier target variable is derived from four operational signals:

SignalConditionWeight
requires_road_closure== True+2
priority== High+1
event_causeaccident / construction / public_event / protest / VIP+1
corridornamed BTP corridor+1
Score 1–2 = Low · Score 3 = Medium · Score 4–6 = High.
The high accuracy reflects the model correctly learning this deterministic rule and generalising to unseen event combinations. Real production value lies in inference speed, scale, and the recommendation engine. Ground-truth severity labels from officer assessments should be incorporated in Phase 2.

⚠️ Assumptions & Limitations
